This section is to clarify some thing about this tool.

► What is the tool's purpose?

This tool helps you perform rapidly linear interpolation/extrapolation to save time when performing a full flight calculation usually required on IVAO's ATP part 1 exam.

► How do I use it?

The calculation method varies on the plane's type. About the tool handling, it's pretty easy and intuitive. Some examples are available in the images below.

► Can it be considered cheating?

Definitely not as it's a calculator, by the way, you can use any tool as far as you know to do it properly and hand well the aircraft documentation.

► Is this an official IVAO tool?

No, it isn't, but you can use it at your own risk as a strategic tool for time management on your calculations.

► How is the ATP exam?

Unlike other pilot exams, the ATP exam doesn't have standards at all. However, in part one, English assessment, full flight calculation using only plane's manual, flight instruments, plane's normal/abnormal procedures, and some regulations is usually asked.

► How much knowledge needed for the ATP p1?

Pretty deep on aircraft manuals structure, knowing where everything is on the manuals is essential. A tip for Boeing's choice for evaluation is to take a profound look at the FCOM volume 1 plus 2 and FCTM structure, but not limited to these two (or three) as any aeronautical subject can be discussed extensively.
